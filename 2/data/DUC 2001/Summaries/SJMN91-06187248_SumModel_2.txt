Clarence Thomas, nominee to the Supreme Court, was brought up in segregated Georgia.
He was abandoned by his father and lived with his grandparents who educated him in a Catholic school of nuns.
Thomas became a believer in personal strength and individualism over government activism, in effect, a black conservative.
He opposes abortion rights, quotas and busing.
Thomas was educated at Holy Cross College, graduating in 1971, and Yale Law School in 1974.
He worked as a lawyer in Missouri and was appointed judge of the U.S. Circuit Court of Appeals in 1990.
Thomas is married and has one son.
