The total eclipse on Saturday will be visible in an arc about 125 miles wide from the Northern Baltic Sea and Southeast Finland across the Kola Peninsula and Northeast Soviet Union to the Aleutian Islands.
The Finns are excitedly preparing to view the eclipse.
Joensuu, 300 miles northeast of Helsinki, will be the best viewing location.
An "eclipse secretary" has been appointed to handle preparations in Joensuu.
Finnair, the national airline, has arranged a dozen special flights for eclipse viewers.
Scientists will conduct gravity test measurements of the radius of the sun during the eclipse.
