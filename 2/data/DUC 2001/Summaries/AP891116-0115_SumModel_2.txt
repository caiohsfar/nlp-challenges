The following is a summary of the tornadoes that swept through Huntsville, Alabama and a number of other states on 15 and 16 November, 1989: In Huntsville, rescuers continued to search for victims of a tornado that left 17 dead, 463 injured and 1000 homeless.
There was no warning when it hit at rush hour.
Some residents had their own personal accounts of the tornado's fury.
Huntsville's officials were preparing a request for federal aid.
Tornado watches were issued for the Northeast.
Other tornadoes struck over a period of 48 hours in the Mid-Atlantic, Mid-West and Southeast states.
