The official opening of the Channel Tunnel should help the economy of the northwestern regions of Belgium, even though the country's biggest ports will suffer a loss of traffic.
The Flanders region stands to realize the biggest boost from the "chunnel".
The West Flanders Regional Development Authority is preparing to become a part of what is being called the "New European Metropolitan Area".
That will take in the Nord-Pas des Calais in France, Western Flanders and Hainaut in Belgium, and Kent in England.
The biggest advantage for Belgium will come from close cooperation between national authorities and their British and French counterparts.
