It now looks as if the three-tunnel thoroughfare under the English Channel will meet its June 1993 completion date, but it is 60 percent over budget and embroiled in a contractor dispute.
The funding problems are not the only reasons that it is unpopular in England.
The entire project rankles many.
It challenges England's insular identity, and many consider it only as a conduit that will give easy access to all kinds of continental problems, from terrorists to rabies.
Residents in rural and prosperous Kent, where the tunnel will surface, are particularly vocal in their displeasure.
