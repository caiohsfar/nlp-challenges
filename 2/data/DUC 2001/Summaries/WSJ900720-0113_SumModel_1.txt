As Alberto Fujimori prepares to take over as Peru's next president on July 28, the question of whether the Shining Path guerrilla movement will hound his administration as it has the previous two ranks high among his concerns.
Among Shining Path watchers, there is strong disagreement on fundamentals: Is the Shining Path getting stronger or weaker?
Is it making progress or losing ground among the population?
And finally, who is actually winning the war?
Mr. Fujimori will have his work cut out for him when he takes office.
The insurgents have followed Shining Path Founder Abimael Guzman with the commitment that the faithful reserve for their prophet.
