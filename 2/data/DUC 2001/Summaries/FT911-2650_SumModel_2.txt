When Lewis Preston takes over as president of the World Bank this September, he will have to find a way to best promote development in the Third World.
The bank has not had an effective president since the 1980s when Robert McNamara headed the institution.
Grinding poverty is a near universal condition in much of the world where 1 billion people live on less than one dollar a day.
In the past decade Third World countries have gone backwards.
Real per capita incomes have declined substantially in sub-Saharan Africa and the Middle East, and mildly in Latin America.
