The pledge to "end welfare as we know it" and the two-year limit on welfare checks were pre-election themes voiced by President Clinton.
Americans view welfare as an evil of society and there is considerable pressure to reduce it.
Two congressional actions may reduce welfare rolls and shift people off welfare by making work pay.
Many conservatives favor a time limit for welfare.
They feel this will change behavior.
Strict measures for timely welfare reduction may be too harsh.
Conversely, a more gradual and humane phase-in would take years.
Perhaps the goals set in the first instance were too high.
