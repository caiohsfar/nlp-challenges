Rain and higher humidity helped firefighters control blazes in six western states.
Firefighters in Nebraska began seeding grass and planting trees.
However, the return of hot and dry weather will soon end this welcomed break.
In New Mexico, a lightening- caused fire polluted a creek with ash.
Biologists there rescued hundreds of the endangered species of Gila trout.
566 trout were caught with long-handled nets and taken to the Mescalero Fish Hatchery, where they will remain until the creek becomes ash free.
Officials hope at least 90% will survive.
Elsewhere, an arson-caused fire blackened much of California's scenic coastline near Big Sur.
