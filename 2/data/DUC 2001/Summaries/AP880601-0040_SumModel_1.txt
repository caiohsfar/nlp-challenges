Typically, Atlantic hurricane season is June 1-Nov 30, with six of 10 named tropical storms becoming hurricanes with drenching rain and wind over 74 mph.
1987 had three hurricanes and four tropical storms.
Hurricane Emily, the fastest moving hurricane in this century, hit the Dominican Republic on September 22nd with wind gusts up to 110 mph, killing three.
It recovered enough to belt Bermuda with 116 mph winds three days later.
Hurricane Arlene meandered through the Atlantic in mid-August with top winds of 75 mph.
Hurricane Floyd hit Key West with 75 mph winds but fizzled out over the Everglades and Miami in mid-October.
