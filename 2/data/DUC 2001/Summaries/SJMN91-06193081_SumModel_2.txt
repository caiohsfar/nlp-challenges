From Hawaii to Mexico and on to Central America more that 40 million people saw the moon totally darken the sun.
When the eclipse was at its height, the Mauna Loa volcano erupted for the first time in six tears, and released a dazzling display of lava.
High cirrus clouds caused the failure of three of the ten experiments carried out in the observatory on top of 13,800 foot Mauna Kee.
High cloud cover dimmed the view in some areas, but those who got a good look marveled at the celestial display.
