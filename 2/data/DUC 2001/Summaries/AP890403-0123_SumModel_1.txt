According to the National Weather Service and other sources, most tornadoes occur between 3 and 6 PM and travel from southwest to northeast.
A thunderstorm next to large hail is where tornadoes originate.
In 1988, 32 deaths occurred, well below the average of 99 per year.
Only two percent of all tornadoes are violent with speeds of 200 m.p.h.
They travel only an average of 26 miles but the longest one traveled 219 miles in 1925.
Forward speeds are between 30-70 m.p.h.
The largest outbreak on record was in 1974 when 148 storms killed 300 people in 13 states.
