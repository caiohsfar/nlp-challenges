The mid-west's worsening drought is hurting what started as one of the most optimistic farm years in the last eight.
Although crop prices are now soaring on weather-worries, yields may be so low that many farmers will not do well.
All farm related industries such as bankers, exporters, realtors, investors and equipment manufactures may be affected.
Inflation concerns are driving stock and bond prices lower.
Nationwide, pasture conditions are the worst they have been since 1934.
The government has authorized hay mowing on Conservation Reserve Program lands, but warns that it may have to curb its agriculture "export enhancement" program because supplies are unpredictable.
