Louisiana began a massive rebuilding effort after Hurricane Andrew flattened whole districts, killing two people and injuring dozens.
Main industries such as oysters, alligators, cotton and sugar cane are threatened; however, the oil industry was barely affected.
The Federal Emergency Management Agency is setting aside $77 million to help.
Meanwhile, local officials in Florida, where 15 people died, are critical of the delay in supplying food, drinking water and supplies for thousands in need.
One in eight Dade County residents lost their homes, approximately a quarter of a million people.
Florida damage is in the $20-30 billion range.
President Bush has ordered troops to Florida.
