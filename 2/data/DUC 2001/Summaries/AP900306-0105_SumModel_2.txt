Clarence Thomas was confirmed as a federal appeals judge on 6 March 1990.
He will be a judge on the U.S. Circuit Court of Appeals in the District of Columbia.
Complaints from liberals and senior citizens were ignored during the confirmation.
Thomas was chairman of the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission under Reagan and Bush.
He is a critic of affirmative action and quotas.
Thomas was born into poverty in Georgia.
He worked his way through college and completed Yale law school.
Before his tenure on the Commission, he worked for Senator Danforth, a strong Thomas supporter, as a lawyer.
