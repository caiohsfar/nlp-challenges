A small number of gem quality diamonds have been discovered in Canada's Northwest Territories.
Activity there is hectic, but it is unlikely that the world's diamond market will notice.
The large number of illegally mined diamonds reaching the market from Angola has disturbed the De Beers' marketing arm.
They are buying up as many Angolan diamonds as they possibly can to stabilize prices when demand is depressed.
The De Beers-controlled cartel has survived by controlling prices, partly because diamonds are not essential.
Diamonds of gem quality are prized for their show value, but there are ready substitutes for diamonds used in industry.
