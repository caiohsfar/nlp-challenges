Exxon Corp. increased its estimate of the total 1989 costs of cleaning up the massive Alaskan oil spill to $2 billion dollars and another $500 million will be taken in the fourth quarter for cleanup costs.
The figure does not include liability from more than 150 lawsuits that Exxon faces from the spill.
The fourth quarter charge is in addition to $880 million set aside in the first and second quarters, bring Exxon's total cleanup costs for the year to $1.38 billion.
Exxon's size is said to insulate it from serious long-term financial damage.
Exxon's stock could suffer from continuing negative publicity.
