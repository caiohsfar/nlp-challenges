When Margaret Thatcher and Francois Mitterrand signed the tunnel treaty in February 1986, eggs were thrown Mitterrand's automobile.
This was a harbinger of the disgust that would be expressed later by many English when asked about the "chunnel".
It may be Europe's biggest civil-engineering project of the century, as it is so advertised, but now, four years after it was authorized, public opinion polls show that the English people don't want a fixed link to the continent.
They are especially critical of the French.
Despite the opposition, transport analysts say the tunnel will be a boon to British industry.
