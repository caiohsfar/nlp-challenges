The "solar eclipse of the century" is still six months away, but Mexico's preparations are at full speed.
A cabinet-level commission, "eclipse 1991", has been named to handle preparations.
Most hotels are sold out.
Four ships will act as floating hotels.
The 165 mile-wide eclipse path will reach from Hawaii through Mexico to Brazil, and pass through one of the world's most populous regions.
It will last seven minutes, the maximum length for a total solar eclipse.
An eclipse is illusionary.
The sun is 400 times larger than the moon, but it seems to cover the moon because it is 400 times closer to earth.
