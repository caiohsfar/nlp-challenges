Dean became a hurricane Wednesday as it moved through the eastern Caribbean at 20 mph, with 80 mph winds by nightfall.
At midnight it was moving westerly at 15 mph, its latest position 65 miles NE of Barbuda, 240 miles east of St. Thomas.
Currently small, with hurricane-force winds confined to a 25 mile-radius, it could strengthen dangerously in the next 24 hours.
It was expected to hit land at Barbuda Wednesday night and move toward the US Virgin Islands and Puerto Rico over the next 24-36 hours.
