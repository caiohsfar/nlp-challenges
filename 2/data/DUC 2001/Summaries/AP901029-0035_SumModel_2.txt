In Kansas City, Terry D. Barnes, 24, was shot between the eyes in his own apartment when police mistook him for a burglar.
This incident is one more strike against a police force already struggling with allegations of brutality and racism.
In June, several white officers bloodied a Nigerian-born Roman Catholic priest with nightsticks as he lay on the ground.
In May, another black clergyman was hit on the head with a shotgun by a white officer while lying on the ground.
Police Chief Steven Bishop said that the department's standing has fallen in the community, and a "behavior modification" program is being developed for offending officers.
